<p><p>RNA interference (RNAi) is a biological process used in therapeutics to silence specific genes and modulate disease pathways. The market comprises three main types: small interfering RNA (siRNA), which directly targets mRNA for degradation; microRNA (miRNA), which regulates gene expression at the post-transcriptional level; and short hairpin RNA (shRNA), which is a synthetic RNA that can be processed into functional siRNA within cells. Each type has unique mechanisms and applications for treating various diseases, including cancer and genetic disorders.</p></p>
