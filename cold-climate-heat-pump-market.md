<p><p>The cold-climate heat pump market includes two primary types: ductless heat pumps and central ducted heat pumps. Ductless heat pumps, or mini-splits, operate without ductwork, offering flexibility and individual room control, making them ideal for retrofits in homes. Central ducted heat pumps, on the other hand, utilize existing duct systems to distribute heated or cooled air throughout the entire home, providing efficient climate control for larger spaces. Both types are designed to operate effectively in low temperatures, ensuring year-round comfort.</p></p>
