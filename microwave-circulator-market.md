<p><p>The microwave circulator market consists of passive, three-port, and four-port types. Passive circulators are non-amplifying devices that direct microwave signals among ports without power supply. Three-port circulators allow signal flow in one direction, improving isolation between ports, commonly used in radar and telecommunication systems. Four-port circulators offer even greater flexibility, enabling multiple connections and enhanced signal routing. These devices are crucial in various applications, including satellite communication, wireless networks, and industrial RF systems, ensuring efficient signal management.</p></p>
